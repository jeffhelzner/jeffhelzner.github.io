About & Research Focus

I am a former academic philosopher who has been working in the insurance industry since 2014. My main research interests focus on improving decision-making by developing a better understanding of how decision makers, whether human or AI, fail to meet relevant standards of rational choice and decision quality. My work spans academia and industry, blending formal models with empirical methods and engineering practice to improve how decision makers form beliefs, select actions, and update in light of new evidence.

The projects featured here will focus on AI alignment through the lens of rationality and consistency: evaluating models against norms of coherent belief, calibrated uncertainty, robust reasoning, and stable preferences; designing diagnostics and interventions that improve decision quality; and building practical tooling to make alignment claims testable.
