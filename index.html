About me

I'm a former philosophy professor with an interest in logic, rational choice, and uncertainty. For the past decade or so, I've been working to improve decision-making in the insurance industry. It's a great industry for anyone interested in decision-making. Despite this steady flow of interesting applied problems, I maintain an interest in more philosophical questions. The projects here are mainly intended to serve those interests.

Explorations

Here are some of the questions that i hope to explore here:

  • What is a rational decision? How do I know if you've made one? How do I know if I've made one?
  • What is a rational decision maker? Is a rational decision maker simply a decision maker who usually (always) makes rational decisions?
  • What's the relationship between good decisions and rational ones? Are good decisions simply decisions that are sufficiently close to being rational ones?
